The Ritual · No. III

A single drop,
slowly.

Ittar is not perfume in a hurry. There are four small things to know, and then it is yours.

№ I

Pulse points.

Apply a single drop to the wrists, the hollow of the neck, or behind the ears. Ittar is concentrated — many times more than alcoholic perfume. One drop, divided across two pulse points, carries through the day. More is not better; more is too much.

№ II

Warm, don't rub.

Press the wrists gently together for a beat, then let go. Rubbing breaks the top notes — the lightest molecules — and shortens the life of the scent. The warmth of the skin alone is enough to open the fragrance. This is the most common mistake. The fix is simple: be still for a moment.

№ III

Set an intention.

This is the part most perfumes leave out. Pause for a breath after applying. Name the thing you are walking toward — courage, calm, clarity, presence; whatever your sign asks for that day. The scent becomes a quiet anchor. When you catch it later, you remember what you set out to do.

№ IV

Store with care.

Keep the bottle upright, capped tightly, in a cool place away from sunlight and heat. A drawer is ideal. A windowsill is not. Stored well, an ittar does not fade — it deepens, the way a good wine does. A 12 ml bottle, used as a single-drop daily ritual, lasts roughly three to four months.

A closing thought

The point is not the perfume.
The point is the pause
you take to put it on.
